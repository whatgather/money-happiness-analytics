# Money, Happiness & Quality of Life Analytics

## Overview
This project explores the relationship between economic, behavioral, and social factors associated with happiness and quality of life across countries.

Using global happiness datasets from 2015–2019, this analysis investigates how variables such as GDP, freedom, social support, and life expectancy interact with reported happiness scores.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze factors associated with happiness
- Explore relationships between income and life satisfaction
- Build predictive models using behavioral and economic indicators
- Generate insights from global quality-of-life data

---

## Tools Used
- Python
- pandas
- NumPy
- matplotlib
- scikit-learn

---

## Key Insights
- Higher GDP alone does not fully predict happiness.
- Social support and freedom consistently showed strong relationships with happiness.
- Countries balancing economic stability and autonomy ranked highest overall.
- Behavioral and social indicators often predicted happiness more strongly than income alone.
